Est. 1962

Central Letterpress opened when the Graphic Communications Union negotiated shop rights on Cermak Road. The original owner, Władysław "Walt" Kozłowski, installed a Vandercook No. 4 and a Chandler & Price 12×18 in a space that still has the same concrete floor.

Walt's daughter Marta runs the shop now. She learned to set type at twelve. She has no interest in digital presses, laser cutters, or "hybrid workflows." The Vandercooks work. The type is clean. The impression is real.

We are proud members of the Printing, Publishing and Media Workers Sector of the Communication Workers of America, AFL-CIO.

"Ink on paper is not a nostalgic act. It is a permanent one."

— Marta Kozłowski, proprietor
