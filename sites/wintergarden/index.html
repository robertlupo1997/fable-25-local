The Conservatory

Founded in the tradition of the great Victorian glasshouses, the Wintergarden Conservatory maintains a living collection no two visitors will see alike.

Each garden is seeded by a unique number — a stochastic grammar that encodes the branching logic of living plants. Stems extend branch by branch in real time, governed by axiomatic rules: the fern unfurls by repeated bifurcation; the hibiscus builds alternating lateral branches, saving its bloom for the final recursion; the reed climbs nearly straight, its leaves unwrapping at each node.

These are not photographs or pre-rendered scenes. They are performed, each time, for you.

Cultivation Method

Recursive stochastic L-systems at depth 4–5, parameterised by species-specific grammars. Each stem is a quadraticCurveTo bezier on HTML canvas with tapered lineWidth — the base carries full caliper; each generation thins by a fixed factor. Leaves are filled bezier paths rotated to the branch angle. Blooms are elliptical petals arranged radially with per-petal PRNG jitter. Chimes: pentatonic sine oscillators in the Web Audio API, muted until invited.
