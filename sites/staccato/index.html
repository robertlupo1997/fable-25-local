THE MACHINE

No samples. No shortcuts. Every sound is oscillators and noise shaped by amplitude envelopes.

A kick drum is a 180 Hz sine swept to 40 Hz in 350 milliseconds — the same physics as a speaker-cone impulse. The snare is bandpass-filtered white noise stacked on a decaying triangle oscillator. The hi-hat is high-passed chaos above 8 kHz, lasting 50 milliseconds or less. The tom is a deeper fall, slower pocket, 100 to 50 Hz in half a second.

The analyser at the top reads the live audio frame and draws what the air would look like if you could see it. The machine schedules sound 100 ms ahead — so your ears hear it exactly on the grid, not a frame late.
