Every June, the meadow becomes the sky's living room.

Since 1978, the fields above Bramblefell have filled with two hundred kites at once — box kites, delta wings, fighters on invisible strings, and a few contraptions that openly defy categorisation. The festival runs across the last full weekend in June, with free entry to the meadow from first light to last.

We are run entirely by volunteers, funded by the flying community, and deeply opposed to any kite with a branded logo on it.

Under-14 category. Any material, any design. Measured from nose to tip. Winner takes the Bramblefell Cup.

Food vendors in the lower meadow. The Hatch serves pulled pork and flatbreads. Kites may remain aloft unattended at your own risk.

The main event. Judged on construction, altitude, and the quality of the flier's conversation.

17:30
Synchronised Ballet

Six pilots, one sky, a twelve-minute piece set to Ravel's string quartet. Attendance last year was 1,400. Arrive early.

20:00
Lantern Lift

Lit kites at dusk. The meadow goes quiet. Strings glow. This is the one that makes people return every year.

Practical Notes

Bring Your Kit

  • Delta kites handle the meadow's crosswind well. Avoid box kites on gusty days — the turbulence behind the ridge makes them unpredictable.
  • String: 80 lb test minimum. The field record holder used 200 metres of 150 lb Dyneema. He also lost the kite.
  • Leave the carbon spars at home if there's any rain in the forecast. Fibreglass is heavier but you'll be glad of it.
  • Dogs are welcome on a lead. The kite fights are not, technically, dogfights.
  • Parking is limited on the access road. The field is a 20-minute walk from Bramblefell station.

The meadow is operated as a nature reserve. Please carry out everything you bring in. String tails found after dark become the festival's problem and yours.

What we watch for

  • Wind 10–30 km/h ideal. Below 10 and nothing flies. Above 45 and the field is closed.
  • Rain A shower won't stop us. A thunderstorm will. Check the festival account the morning of.
  • Visibility Under 2 km: mandatory grounding of all kites above 100 m altitude.
