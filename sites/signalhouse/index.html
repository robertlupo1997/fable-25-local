WHAT ARE NUMBERS STATIONS?

Numbers stations are shortwave radio transmissions of unknown origin that broadcast sequences of numbers, letters, phonetic alphabets, or coded tones. First documented during World War I, they proliferated during the Cold War as a secure method for intelligence agencies to communicate with field operatives.

The one-time pad system means that without the matching key, these transmissions are theoretically unbreakable. The agent receives the broadcast, decodes it with their personal pad, and destroys the page. No record. No intercept. No proof.

This archive monitors the shortwave bands for active transmissions. Many stations documented here have broadcast continuously for decades with no official acknowledgment of their existence.

This receiver is a simulation. All transmissions are procedurally synthesized from authentic formats. Station callsigns and characteristics are based on documented records compiled by the Conet Project and shortwave monitoring community since 1992.
