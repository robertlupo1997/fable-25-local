A face with four voices.

Variable from whim
to authority.

Fraunces is a display typeface with a singular character: deeply optical, richly variable, unafraid of personality.

Four axes span the full spectrum of intent — from ultralight captions to assertive black headlines, from soft rounded glyphs to angular precision, from sober geometry to deliberate eccentricity. One face; every register.

At small optical sizes the letterforms open: wider apertures, adjusted stroke contrast, subtly shifted proportions — each change in service of readability. At display scale, the serifs sharpen, proportions tighten, and the face becomes unmistakably itself.
